Week-wise Theory Notes

Week 1 - Introduction to Industrial Engineering

Focus: evolution, objectives, tools of IE, organizational basics.

Quick notes: IE improves productivity through better methods, resource use, and quality. Core tools include method study, work measurement, PPC, quality tools, OR techniques.

Week 2 - Organizational Structure and Roles

Focus: functional/product/process structures, roles and culture.

Quick notes: Structure defines authority, responsibility, and communication. Tall structures have many layers; flat structures have wider spans of control.

Week 3 - Plant Location: Site Selection

Focus: location factors and selection methods.

Quick notes: Typical factors: raw material, labor, market, transport, utilities, taxes. Common methods: factor rating, point rating, break-even comparison, center of gravity.

Week 4 - Plant Layout Fundamentals

Focus: purpose and types of layouts.

Quick notes: Product layout suits high-volume standardized products; process layout suits variety/low volume; cellular layout groups similar part families.

Week 5 - Layout Design and Facility Organization

Focus: process/product layout design and material flow.

Quick notes: Good layout reduces handling, waiting, congestion, and lead time. Facility organization links man-machine-material for smooth throughput.

Week 6 - Material Handling and PPC Scope

Focus: handling principles and production planning/control basics.

Quick notes: Material handling should minimize non-value-added movement. PPC covers planning, routing, loading, scheduling, dispatching, and follow-up.

Week 7 - PPC Details: Routing, Scheduling, Sequencing

Focus: MRP, routing, priority rules, shop scheduling.

Quick notes: Routing decides operation path; scheduling decides timing; sequencing decides order. Priority rules include FCFS, SPT, EDD, CR.

Week 8 - Inventory Models

Focus: inventory fundamentals and deterministic models.

Quick notes: EOQ (Wilson model) balances ordering and holding cost. Reorder level depends on demand and lead time. Gradual replenishment model applies when production and consumption happen simultaneously.

Week 9 - Project Management, PERT, CPM Basics

Focus: network modelling and critical path concepts.

Quick notes: Critical path has longest duration and zero slack. PERT handles uncertain activity times with expected time and variance; crashing reduces project duration at added direct cost.

Week 10 - CPM and Forecasting Introduction

Focus: network analysis and forecasting methods.

Quick notes: Forecasting methods include moving average, weighted moving average, exponential smoothing, and trend-based models. Forecast error metrics guide method selection.

Week 11 - Forecasting Accuracy and Quality Tools

Focus: MAE/MSE/MAPE, tracking signal, QC tools.

Quick notes: MAE averages absolute error, MSE penalizes larger errors. Fishbone identifies causes; Pareto prioritizes vital few causes.

Week 12 - SPC, Control Charts, Work Study

Focus: variable and attribute charts, productivity and work study.

Quick notes: Variables charts: X-bar, R; attributes charts: p, np, c, u. Method study improves process method; work measurement sets standard time.
